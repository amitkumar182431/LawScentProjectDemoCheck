Corporate Law

By Senior Advocate

Corporate law governs the formation, functioning, and regulation of companies, ensuring transparency, accountability, and protection of stakeholders. In India, this area of law is primarily regulated by the Companies Act, 2013, which replaced the Companies Act, 1956 and became effective from 1 April 2014.

Corporate Governance & Compliance

The Companies Act, 2013 introduced stricter corporate governance norms, including mandatory board meetings, independent directors, audit committees, and enhanced disclosure requirements. Directors are legally bound to act in good faith and in the best interests of the company and its shareholders.

Legal Proof & Judicial Interpretation

Sections 166 and 184 of the Companies Act, 2013 clearly define the duties and disclosure obligations of directors. Non-compliance can attract civil and criminal penalties.

In the landmark case of Sahara India Real Estate Corp. Ltd. v. SEBI (2012), the Supreme Court of India held that funds raised from the public must comply with regulatory and disclosure requirements, reinforcing investor protection.

Specialized forums such as the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) and National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) adjudicate corporate disputes, insolvency matters, and cases of oppression and mismanagement.
