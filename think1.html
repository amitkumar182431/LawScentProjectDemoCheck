Supreme Court SOP on Argument Time

By Senior Advocate

The Supreme Court of India has recently introduced a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) concerning the time taken by lawyers during oral arguments. This move reflects the Court's growing concern over delays caused by prolonged hearings and the uneven use of judicial time. The SOP aims to bring discipline, predictability, and fairness to court proceedings without imposing rigid restrictions.

Background and Purpose

The Supreme Court has repeatedly observed that judicial time is a limited public resource and must be used responsibly. The new SOP is an attempt to address this issue through better planning rather than strict enforcement.

Key Features of the SOP

One of the main requirements under the SOP is that lawyers must indicate in advance how much time they expect to take for oral arguments. This information must be submitted before the hearing, allowing the Court to manage its daily cause list more effectively.

Additionally, the SOP encourages lawyers to file short written submissions ahead of time. These written notes are meant to be concise and focused, helping judges understand the core issues before the hearing begins. This reduces the need for repetitive oral arguments in court.

No Fixed Time Limit, Only Accountability

Importantly, the SOP does not prescribe a mandatory or fixed time limit for arguments. Instead, it relies on self-declaration by lawyers. The responsibility lies with the counsel to realistically assess the time needed and to adhere to the timeline they have proposed.

This approach reflects trust in the legal profession and promotes self-regulation. However, it also sends a clear message that unnecessary delays will no longer be viewed lightly.

Expected Impact on Court Functioning

If followed sincerely, the SOP can significantly reduce part-heard matters and repeated adjournments. It can help courts hear more cases in a day and ensure that urgent matters receive timely attention. Structured arguments may also improve the overall quality of advocacy by encouraging clarity and precision.

From the judges' perspective, advance written submissions allow better preparation, leading to more focused questions and efficient hearings.

Challenges and Concerns

While the SOP is a positive step, its success depends largely on cooperation from lawyers. Over-estimation or under-estimation of argument time could affect scheduling. There are also concerns about whether judges will strictly enforce adherence to declared timelines.

Another challenge is the need for adequate institutional support. For shorter hearings to be effective, judges must have sufficient research assistance and time to study the written submissions in advance.

Conclusion

The Supreme Court's SOP on argument timelines represents a balanced approach to judicial reform. Instead of imposing strict limits, it promotes responsibility, preparation, and respect for judicial time. If implemented earnestly by both the Bench and the Bar, this initiative could play an important role in improving the efficiency and accessibility of the justice system in India.

Historical Context & Precedents

The Supreme Court has long grappled with the challenge of balancing advocacy rights with judicial efficiency. Previous attempts to regulate courtroom time have included informal guidelines and ad-hoc measures, but this SOP represents a more systematic approach to time management in the legal system.

The Court has cited various instances where important cases have been delayed due to extended oral arguments in less urgent matters. This new SOP is expected to create a more structured approach to judicial proceedings while maintaining the fundamental right to adequate representation.
