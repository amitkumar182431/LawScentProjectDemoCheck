Criminal Justice

By High Court Judge

The criminal justice system in India is designed to protect society while safeguarding individual liberty. It is rooted in the principles of fairness, due process, and judicial accountability, as guaranteed under Articles 14, 20, 21, and 22 of the Constitution of India, which came into force on 26 January 1950.

Fair Trial & Due Process

A fair trial is a fundamental right under Article 21. It includes the presumption of innocence, the right to legal representation, the right to be informed of charges, and protection against arbitrary arrest. Criminal proceedings in India are governed procedurally by the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.

Judicial Discretion & Landmark Judgments

Judicial discretion plays a vital role in bail, sentencing, and trial management. In Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India (1978), the Supreme Court expanded the scope of Article 21, holding that procedure established by law must be just, fair, and reasonable.

In Hussainara Khatoon v. State of Bihar (1979), the Supreme Court recognized the right to speedy trial as an essential component of fair justice, leading to the release of thousands of undertrial prisoners.

Courts ensure balance between public interest and individual rights, ensuring that punishment is proportionate and justice is neither delayed nor denied.
